Both values are compelling, but they conflict directly in this scenario. Approving the housing project prioritizes immediate human need over long-term environmental health, while protecting the wetlands prioritizes ecological integrity over urgent social demands. Additionally, there’s a question of whose needs take precedence—current residents versus future generations or non-human species.

After careful consideration, I would vote to protect the wetlands. My reasoning hinges on prioritizing environmental stewardship as the paramount value in this context. The destruction of a unique habitat is a permanent loss, not only for the local ecosystem but for the broader fight against biodiversity collapse, which threatens all life, including human life, in the long run. While the housing shortage is a critical issue, the funding can potentially be redirected elsewhere, whereas the wetlands, once destroyed, cannot be restored. I am willing to sacrifice the immediate alleviation of human need in this specific instance because the principle of intergenerational responsibility outweighs the short-term utilitarian gain.
